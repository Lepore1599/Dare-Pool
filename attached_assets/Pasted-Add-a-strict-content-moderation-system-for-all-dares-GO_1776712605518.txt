Add a strict content moderation system for all dares.

GOAL:
Automatically block or flag unsafe, illegal, harmful, offensive, hateful, abusive, or inappropriate dares before they are posted, while keeping the platform fun and safe.

REQUIREMENTS:

1. HARD REJECT / BLOCKED CONTENT
Do NOT allow dares that include or encourage any of the following:
- Self-harm, suicide, injury, starvation, choking, cutting, overdose, or anything physically dangerous
- Dangerous stunts or reckless behavior that could cause serious harm
- Illegal activity such as theft, vandalism, trespassing, drugs, weapons misuse, fraud, or property damage
- Violence toward other people or animals
- Harassment, bullying, threats, intimidation, stalking, humiliation, or targeting specific individuals/groups
- Sexual or explicit content
- Anything involving minors in unsafe, exploitative, or inappropriate situations
- Racism, slurs, hate speech, or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, gender, sexuality, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Offensive, abusive, degrading, or vulgar language meant to attack or demean others
- Extremist, hateful, or dehumanizing language
- Disguised, censored, symbol-substituted, or misspelled versions of slurs, hateful terms, or offensive language

If detected:
- Prevent submission immediately
- Show message: “This dare violates community guidelines and cannot be posted.”

2. SOFT WARNING / FLAGGED CONTENT
For borderline dares that may be risky, rude, or socially inappropriate but not severe enough for an automatic block:
- Show warning popup:
  “This dare may be risky or inappropriate. Please revise it or confirm if you want to continue.”
- Let the user either edit the dare or confirm before posting

Examples of soft-flag cases:
- Mild prank dares
- Socially awkward or embarrassing dares
- Language that is questionable but not clearly hateful or dangerous

3. IMPLEMENTATION
- Check both the dare title and description
- Use keyword filtering with separate arrays/lists for:
  - banned terms/phrases
  - flagged terms/phrases
- Make filtering case-insensitive
- Detect partial matches where appropriate
- Normalize text before checking by:
  - converting to lowercase
  - removing punctuation and extra spaces
  - replacing common leetspeak / symbol substitutions (example: @ -> a, 1 -> i/l, 3 -> e, 5 -> s, 0 -> o)
  - stripping repeated symbols/spaces meant to bypass filters
- Include logic to catch simple obfuscation and misspellings of harmful or offensive words
- Do not expose the exact blocked keywords to the user

4. SAFETY + EXTENSIBILITY
- Keep moderation rules organized and easy to update later
- Store banned and flagged keywords in clearly labeled arrays/constants
- Add placeholder comments for future upgrades such as:
  - AI moderation
  - admin review system
  - trust/safety dashboard
  - smarter context-based moderation
- Structure the code so moderation can be reused anywhere dares are created or edited

5. USER EXPERIENCE
- Keep messages neutral, simple, and non-accusatory
- Use:
  - “This dare violates community guidelines and cannot be posted.”
  - “This dare may be risky or inappropriate. Please revise it or confirm if you want to continue.”
- Do not reveal which exact word or phrase triggered the moderation

6. REPORT SYSTEM
Add a “Report Dare” button on each dare.
Users should be able to report a dare for reasons such as:
- Dangerous / self-harm
- Illegal activity
- Harassment / bullying
- Hate speech / racism
- Sexual / inappropriate
- Offensive language
- Other

When a dare is reported:
- Mark it as reported
- Hide it or flag it for review
- Prevent it from being featured until reviewed

7. GOAL
Make the platform feel fun, competitive, and creative while preventing harmful, illegal, hateful, abusive, or offensive dares from being created or spread.
